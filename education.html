The world as it is

Teaching is the job. The paperwork is the other job.

The teacher shortage isn't just about pay. It's about the weight of a system that asks its best people to be educators, administrators, data analysts, and compliance officers — simultaneously, every day.

Lesson planning. Progress reports. IEP documentation. Parent communications. Curriculum alignment. All of it lands on the same person who also has 30 students waiting for them at 8am.

AI promised to help. Then it required teachers to learn a new tool, manage a new platform, and worry about where their students' data was going.

How Jean works

Three steps. Then it just gets you.

Jean learns how you teach — your voice, your students, your standards. It doesn't replace your judgment. It amplifies what you already know.

01

It runs on your device

Jean installs locally. No student data ever touches a cloud server. FERPA compliance isn't a policy — it's a property of the architecture. It cannot leak what it never sends.

02

It learns your voice

Jean builds a model of how you communicate — your tone, your curriculum approach, your students' needs. Lesson plans, parent communications, and reports sound like you, not a machine.

03

You get your evenings back

Tasks that used to take two hours take twenty minutes. The work that only a teacher can do — connecting, inspiring, seeing a student — gets more of your time and energy.
